## What is A2A Commerce?

**A2A Commerce (Agent-to-Agent Commerce)** is the fifth paradigm of electronic commerce, following B2B, B2C, C2C, and O2O. In A2A Commerce, both the buyer and the seller are AI Agents acting on behalf of human principals, autonomously executing the full transaction lifecycle — from discovery and negotiation to settlement and evaluation.

> **Traditional e-commerce**: humans use tools to buy and sell.
> **AI-assisted e-commerce**: humans instruct AI to help buy and sell.
> **A2A Commerce**: humans delegate to Agents that autonomously buy and sell.

## Three Essential Properties

A platform qualifies as A2A Commerce only if **all three** conditions are satisfied:

1. **Agent-Native** — APIs, data, and interactions are designed for Agents as first-class consumers, not as an afterthought on human interfaces.
2. **Bilateral Autonomy** — Both the buyer side and the seller side are represented by autonomous Agents, not just one side.
3. **Delegated Authority** — Agents operate within boundaries set by human principals, who retain ultimate decision rights.

## Why Publish This?

A2A Commerce represents a structural shift in how online markets operate. As AI Agents become the primary interface between people and digital services, the existing paradigms (B2C, C2C, B2B) break down — they were designed for humans clicking buttons, not for Agents negotiating deals.

This specification exists to:

- **Establish shared vocabulary** for a new domain, so builders, researchers, and regulators can talk about the same things
- **Prevent fragmentation** by offering a neutral reference implementation of concepts
- **Invite collaboration** from Agent providers, merchants, developers, and academics

We believe A2A Commerce will become as foundational as "SaaS" or "Web 2.0" — a term everyone uses because there is a clear thing to name.
